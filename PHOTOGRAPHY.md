# PASHM — Photography Brief

A shot-by-shot spec for the homepage and supply pages. Hand this to your photographer.
The goal: a single, coherent set that looks **shot by one hand** — quiet, warm, tactile.
Reference register: Cariaggi, Loro Piana, Kinfolk — *not* e-commerce product shots.

---

## The look (every frame obeys this)

- **Mood:** quiet, warm, natural. Light does the work; nothing styled-looking or "commercial."
- **Palette:** stay inside the brand's range — bone, stone, ink, and the undyed fibre colours
  (cream → fawn → brown → charcoal). **No competing colours** in props, walls, or wardrobe.
- **Tonality:** soft, slightly muted. Gentle contrast. **No** crushed blacks, **no** blown
  highlights, **no** punchy saturation, **no** HDR, **no** cool/blue cast.
- **Deliver NEUTRAL masters.** The website applies its own subtle grade on top
  (≈ −20% saturation, soft contrast, slightly warm). So shoot/expose *clean and natural* and
  do **not** heavily stylise in post — a flat, accurate file lets the site's grade land
  consistently across the whole set. Over-graded files will double up and look off.

---

## Technical baseline

| | Spec |
|---|---|
| **Format** | Shoot **RAW**. Full-frame or APS-C. (Phone only as a last resort, and never for macro/product.) |
| **Lenses** | 24–35mm for landscape/environment · 50–85mm for process & product · a true **90–105mm macro** for fibre texture |
| **White balance** | Shoot a **grey card** at the start of each lighting setup; target ~**5000–5200K** for indoor/window work. Embrace warmth (~3500–4200K) only for the golden-hour landscape. |
| **Aperture** | Landscape f/8–f/11 · process/product f/4–f/5.6 · macro f/5.6–f/8 **with focus-stacking** (diffraction softens past ~f/11) · drape/portrait f/2.8–f/4 |
| **Support** | **Tripod** for all macro, product, and still-life. Remote/2-sec timer. Low ISO (100–400). |
| **Deliverables** | High-res masters (TIFF/PNG, long edge ≥ 3000px) **+** web JPEGs (sRGB; long edge ~2400px hero, ~1600px portraits). Shoot **loose** so each frame can crop to both **4:5 portrait** and **16:9 landscape**. |
| **Consistency** | One camera, one WB reference, one grade/preset across the entire set. |

### Lighting kit (natural-light-first, budget-friendly)
You do **not** need a studio. The premium look here comes from *soft, directional* light:
- **A north-facing window / open shade / overcast sky** = a free giant softbox. This is your main light.
- **Diffusion** (a sheet of proper diffusion fabric, or a white bedsheet) to soften direct sun.
- **White foam board** = fill/bounce. **Black foam board / cloth** = negative fill, to deepen
  shadows and add the moody, expensive-looking contrast.
- A **5-in-1 reflector**. Optional: one off-camera flash *only if* you can soften it heavily.

> **If you can only get three frames:** ① the Hero, ② the apple-cider-vinegar wash,
> ③ the macro fibre texture. In that order. They carry the page.

## 1 · HERO — the frame that changes the page
**Used for:** full-bleed homepage hero (`/preview`). **Target file:** `public/photography/hero.jpg`
**Aspect:** shoot 3:2 / wider; will crop to landscape (~16:9). **Text sits bottom-left over a dark scrim** —
so keep the lower-left third compositionally calm and tonally darker; put the subject centre/right/upper.

### Option A — Swat valley landscape *(recommended for "place")*
- **Light:** **Golden hour** — the first hour after sunrise or last before sunset. Sun low, **15–25° above the horizon**. Colour ~**3500–4200K** (warm).
- **Light angle:** **back-side light** — sun roughly behind and to one side of the subject (sun at ~8 or 4 o'clock relative to camera). This rim-lights grass/goats and gives long, soft shadows and depth. A little atmospheric haze/backlit dust is gold.
- **Camera:** eye-level to slightly low; **24–35mm**; **f/8–f/11**; tripod; low ISO. A graduated ND helps hold the sky.
- **Composition:** horizon on the lower or upper third; a foreground lead-in (a path, grass, a comber walking *small* in frame for scale). Leave a quiet, slightly darker zone lower-left for the headline.
- **Background:** mountains/meadow only — **exclude** power lines, vehicles, plastic, modern buildings.
- **Avoid:** flat midday sun, electric-green grass, busy framing.

### Option B — Macro of raw fibre *(strong tactile alternative)*
- **Light:** one **large soft source** (window + diffusion), **raking from the side at ~30–45°** to reveal loft and the tiny shadows between fibres. ~5000K. Add a touch of **backlight** so the fine down **glows** at the edges.
- **Camera:** **90–105mm macro**, **f/4–f/5.6**, tripod, focus on the near loft.
- **Background:** the fibre fills the frame (or on undyed paper). Keep the lower-left soft/dark for text.
- **Avoid:** hard top light (flattens the loft), over-sharpening.

## 2 · SIGNATURE — the apple-cider-vinegar wash
**The image no competitor has. Make it iconic — shoot it the longest.**
**Used for:** process / a tactile moment. **Target file:** `public/photography/the_wash.jpg`. **Aspect:** shoot 4:5 *and* wider.

- **Subject:** hands working the fibre in the wash, **or — the money frame —** the fibre being **lifted, dripping**, out of the liquid.
- **Light:** **soft directional window light from the side (~45°)**, slightly **backlit** to catch the wet sheen, droplets, and any steam. ~**4800–5200K** (neutral, so the vinegar's faint amber reads true).
- **Light shaping:** put a **black flag/board opposite** the window to deepen the shadow side — chiaroscuro = the moody, premium feel. Keep the surroundings dim so hands + wet fibre + liquid are the only bright things.
- **Camera:** **50–85mm**, **f/2.8–f/4** (isolate the hands, soft background). Angle: **30–45° looking down** into the vessel. Shutter **1/250s+** to freeze a drip; or 1/30–1/60s on a tripod for silky water.
- **Background / vessel:** an **authentic vessel** — copper, steel, clay, or aged wood. **No plastic buckets.** Wet stone or worn wood surround.
- **The frame to get:** the lift — fibre rising, water sheeting off, droplets mid-air, one wisp of steam. Shoot dozens; this is the hero of your differentiation.
- **Avoid:** bright flat light, plastic, clutter, a clinical/lab feel.

## 3 · MACRO TEXTURE — how you photograph touch
**Used for:** a tactile interstitial + the supply page. **Target file:** `public/photography/fibre-macro.jpg`. **Aspect:** square / 4:5 and wide.

- **Subject:** a small heap of dehaired down — **and** a version showing the **natural colour gradient** laid in adjacent drifts: cream → fawn → brown → charcoal.
- **Light:** one **large soft source raking very low (~20–30° grazing)** across the fibre — this maximises loft, crimp, and the micro-shadows that read as *softness*. ~5000K. A subtle **backlight/rim** makes the edge fibres halo.
- **Camera:** **90–105mm macro**, **f/5.6–f/8 with focus-stacking** (3–6 frames merged) for sharpness through the loft while the background stays soft. Tripod, low ISO, remote.
- **Background:** the fibre fills the frame, or rests on **undyed handmade paper / raw linen** in the same tone.
- **The frame to get:** the colour-gradient drift, edges glowing, crimp visible. This *is* the cashmere.
- **Avoid:** harsh top light, saturation, sharpening halos.

## 4 · PROCESS SET — Comb · Sort · Wheel · Loom
**Used for:** homepage process section + `/work`. **Target files:** `the_comb.jpg`, `the_sort.jpg`, `the_yarn.jpg`, `the_loom.jpg`. **Aspect:** **4:5 portrait**, shot as a consistent series (same light direction, same grade, hands in focus, faces secondary / no hard direct gaze).

- **The Comb** — hands at the comb, down lifting free of the coat.
  *Light:* side window light **raking (~60–80°)** to show fibre detail. *Camera:* 50–85mm, **f/2.8–f/4**, tight on hands + tool, level or slightly above. *Background:* the goat's coat / soft pasture behind, or the workshop. *Get:* the instant the down releases.
- **The Sort / Dehair** — hands separating fine down from coarse hair.
  *Light:* soft, near-overhead or 45° side; even enough to read both fibre types. *Camera:* **top-down (90°)** or high 3/4, 50mm, **f/4**. *Background:* undyed cloth / aged wood; sorted piles by colour in frame.
- **The Wheel (Yarn)** — the wheel and hands, yarn winding on.
  *Light:* side/back to catch the thin thread and the spokes. *Camera:* 50–85mm, **f/2.8**, profile / 3-4 view; optional **1/15–1/30s** to softly blur the wheel while hands stay sharp (tripod). *Background:* dim workshop, the wheel's wood.
- **The Loom** — weaver, weft in motion.
  *Light:* directional window light **raking across the warp threads** (parallel threads + grazing light = beautiful). *Camera:* 35–50mm for context **plus** a tight detail of the shuttle/weft, **f/4**. *Background:* the loom, warm wood.

For each stage: shoot **one tight detail + one wider context**. Keep the light direction consistent across all four so they read as a set.

## 5 · PRODUCT SET — Fibre · Yarn · Piece
**Used for:** the three-up products row. **Target files:** `fibre.jpg` (or reuse `the_sort.jpg`), `yarn.jpg` (or `the_yarn.jpg`), `shawl.jpg`. **Aspect:** **4:5 portrait**, still-life. **Same surface, same light, same height across all three** so the row is coherent.

- **Fibre piles** — colour-sorted drifts of dehaired down.
  *Light:* **large soft key at ~45°**, low fill, slight **rim/back** to fluff the loft. ~5000K. *Camera:* 50–85mm, **~20–30° above**, **f/5.6–f/8**, tripod. *Background:* undyed paper / raw linen, tone-on-tone, generous negative space.
- **Yarn** — undyed cones or a coiled skein.
  *Light:* soft side light to reveal the twist/ply and matte sheen. *Camera:* 85mm, **f/4–f/5.6**, level or slight above. *Background:* same surface as the fibre shot. *Get:* one cone standing + a loose strand trailing.
- **Finished piece** — a shawl/stole.
  *Light:* large soft window light, **raking slightly** to reveal weave + fringe. *Camera:* 50–85mm, **f/2.8–f/4**. *Background:* daylight interior, neutral wall, wood. *Get:* both a **draped-on-body** (shoulder crop, no face needed) and a **draped-object** (over a chair back / wooden rod), with a **fringe detail**.

## Props & materials checklist
- Undyed handmade paper · raw linen / unbleached cotton · aged/worn wood boards · stone · a lime-washed or plaster wall.
- An **authentic wash vessel** (copper / steel / clay) — no plastic.
- Sorted fibre in all four natural colours · undyed yarn cones or skeins · a finished piece, steamed and lint-free.
- Grey card · tripod · white + black foam boards · diffusion fabric · 5-in-1 reflector · lint roller / tweezers (stray fibres ruin macro).

## Post-processing
- Light, consistent grade across the whole set; deliver **clean neutral masters** (the site adds the final mood — don't double-grade).
- Target feel: warm WB (~5200K), gentle S-curve, **−15–20% saturation**, lifted-but-not-milky blacks, protected highlights. One LUT/preset for the set.
- Spot out stray fibres, dust, modern intrusions. Keep skin/fibre tones natural.

## Suggested shoot order (chase the light)
1. **Golden hour** → Hero landscape (sunrise or sunset block).
2. **Midday, indoors at the window** → the Wash, Macro texture, Product still-lifes (controlled, soft).
3. **Working light** → Process set (comb outdoors/pasture; sort/wheel/loom in the workshop).
